Svetitskhoveli Cathedral - the main patriarchal cathedral of the Georgian Orthodox Church, the mother-cathedral of Georgia, the place of enthronement (enthronement) of the Catholicos-patriarchs of Georgia, the burial place of many medieval Georgian kings, their family members and patriarchs, a monument of Georgian architecture of the 11th century, one of the four from the great cathedrals (Oshki, Bagrat Cathedral, Alaverdi). Svetitskhoveli is located in the city of Mtskheta, 20 kilometers from Tbilisi.

Kobuleti is a town and resort located in Adjara, Kobuleti municipality. It is located on the Kobuleti plain, on the Black Sea coast, 5 meters above sea level. This is a great place for lovers of sea vacations. Here you will find sunny beaches, well-equipped hotels, bars, clubs and restaurants, where you can relax, have fun, enjoy delicious food and spend your time in a variety of ways. Kobuleti is a climatic and balneological resort. The city has a humid subtropical maritime climate with mild, warm winters and hot summers. The average annual temperature is 13.4°C, January - 4.8°C, and July - 22.4°C. Kobuleti received the status of a resort in 1923, and the status of a city in 1944. Beautiful parks, clear blue sea and developed infrastructure create the best environment for an unforgettable vacation. In the city, you can also visit the Kobuleti Museum, where various archeological materials found in the region are presented. Many tourists visit Kobuleti every year.

Sataflia is located in Imereti, in the territory of Gumbra, Banoja and Khomuli villages of Tskaltubo municipality, on Sataflia mountain, 500 meters above sea level. It was established in 1935. The monument is complex in nature and contains geological, paleontological, speleological, zoological and botanical monuments. Visitors can visit fossilized dinosaur footprints, Sataflia karst cave, which is 900 meters long, 10 meters high and 12 meters wide, rock trail, Colchi forest, panoramic views.

Vardzia is a rock-cut monastic ensemble, a monument of Georgian artistic culture of the 12th-13th centuries. It is located in historical Javakheti, Aspindze district (30 km from Aspindze). river on the left bank of Mtkvari. 1300 meters above sea level. According to the orographic scheme, Vardzia is located at the end of the eastern branch of the Erusheti ridge. The extreme upper cave of Vardzia is 1462 m above sea level. The fortress-city of Vardzia is located in tiers at a height of 100 m. The number of tiers is from 3 to 13. It has more than 600 storage rooms. There are dining rooms, canteens, pantries, auxiliary storage rooms and 25 cellars with 185 jars.

Martvili Canyon - a natural monument in Gachedil village, Martvili municipality, Samegrelo region, 210 meters above sea level. The Martville Canyon Visitor Center is located at the entrance to the canyon. The nature monument is a canyon-like gorge carved by the river Abashi in an anticline made of limestone rocks of Abdat. The length of the canyon is 2400 m, the depth of the cut is 20-30 m, and the width is 5-10 m. In the middle of the canyon, there are small waterfalls with a height of 12-15 m. A 700 m circular stone-paved walking path starts from the visitor center and will follow the historic walkers' path. There are 2 bridges, 3 viewing platforms, a 30-step historic staircase built from large limestone boulders. Visitors also have the opportunity to take a boat ride on a 300-meter (one-way) stretch of the Abashi River and enjoy beautiful views of the mountain river canyon. To use the boating route, the height of the visitor must exceed 1 m.

Note:
- For the safety of visitors, access to the navigable section is prohibited in rain and snow due to the rise of the water level.

- Tours are carried out throughout the year. The active season lasts from April to October.

- Visitors also take part in steering the boat together with the boatman.

- 6 people can be accommodated in the boat.
